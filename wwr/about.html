History

Whitewater rafting as a commercial sport began evolving in the 20th century but traces its roots back to early indigenous practices in North America. Native American tribes like the Nez Perce navigated rivers like the Columbia and Snake Rivers for fishing and transportation. The development of modern whitewater rafting started in the mid-1800s, with expeditions such as John C. Fremont’s use of a rubber raft in the Platte River.
